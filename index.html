About Taniti

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Activities & Entertainment

Image Placeholder

Most people visit Taniti to enjoy the beaches, explore the rainforest, and visit the volcano. The island offers a wide range of activities including snorkeling, zip-lining, fishing tours, helicopter rides, museums, art galleries, and nightlife. Many activities are located in Merriton Landing on the north side of Yellow Leaf Bay.

Activities & Pricing

Premium Activities

  • Helicopter Rides: $150-$250 per person
  • Chartered Fishing Tours: $200-$400 per boat
  • Private Boat Tours: $120-$180 per person
  • Zip-lining: $75-$95 per person

Budget Activities

  • Beach Activities: Free
  • Snorkeling: $25-$45 per person
  • Volcano Hikes: $30-$50 (guided) or Free (self-guided)
  • Museum: $8-$12 per person
  • Art Galleries: Free
  • Rainforest Hikes: $35-$55 (guided) or Free
  • Bus Tours: $35-$55 per person
  • Boat Tours: $45-$75 per person

Entertainment

  • Pubs & Microbrewery: $5-$12 per drink
  • Dance Club: $10-$20 cover
  • Movies: $8-$12 per ticket
  • Bowling: $6-$10 per game
  • Golf: $45-$65 per round (opening next year)

Lodging Options

Image Placeholder

Taniti has a wide variety of lodging that ranges from an inexpensive hostel to one large, four-star resort. There are many small, family-owned hotels and a growing number of bed and breakfasts. All types of lodging are strictly regulated and regularly inspected by the Tanitian government.

Accommodation Pricing

  • Four-Star Resort: $250-$400 per night
  • Luxury Bed & Breakfasts: $150-$220 per night
  • Family-Owned Hotels: $60-$100 per night
  • Hostels: $25-$45 per night

All accommodations are regulated and inspected by the Tanitian government. Prices may vary by season.

Restaurants & Dining

Image Placeholder

Taniti currently has 10 restaurants: five serve mostly local fish and rice, three serve American-style meals, and two serve Pan-Asian cuisine.

Restaurant Pricing

  • Fine Dining: $50-$100 per person
  • Casual Local Restaurants: $12-$25 per person
  • American-Style: $15-$30 per person
  • Pan-Asian: $18-$35 per person

Grocery Stores

Taniti has two supermarkets, two smaller grocery stores, and one 24-hour convenience store.

Getting to and Around Taniti

Image Placeholder

Almost all visitors arrive to Taniti by air, though some arrive on a small cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week. Taniti is served by a small airport that can accommodate small jets and propeller planes. Taniti is in the process of expanding the airport so larger jets will be able to land on the island within the next few years.

Getting to Taniti

  • Air Travel: $400-$800 round-trip (varies by season)
  • Cruise Ship: Included in cruise package

Ground Transportation

  • Public Buses (Taniti City): $1.50 per ride (Day pass: $8, Weekly: $35)
  • Private Buses (Island-wide): $3-$8 per ride
  • Taxis: $5-$15 per trip
  • Rental Cars: $35-$75 per day
  • Bike Rentals: $10-$20 per day

Taniti City is very walkable. Public buses run from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. daily.

About Taniti Island

Image Placeholder

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano.

Island Geography

The island features diverse terrain including:

  • Sandy and rocky beaches
  • Small but safe harbor
  • Lush tropical rainforests
  • Mountainous interior with an active volcano

Population & Economy

Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Taniti City

Most tourists spend most of their time in Taniti City, which boasts native architecture and nearby white, sandy beaches that encircle Yellow Leaf Bay.
